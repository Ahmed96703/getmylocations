How to use Street View

  1. Enter an address. Type any address, business name, or landmark. Examples: "1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View", "Big Ben London", "Karachi airport".
  2. Or paste coordinates. A decimal-degree pair like 48.858420, 2.294500 works the same way.
  3. Click View. The Street View image loads in the panel below. Drag to look around; pinch or scroll to zoom; use the arrow overlays to walk forward or back along the road.
  4. Use the My location button to load Street View at your current GPS position.

What is Street View?

Google Street View is a navigable photographic layer over the world's road network. Vehicles equipped with multi-camera rigs (and, in some places, backpack rigs or boats) capture 360° panoramas every few meters along passable roads. Google stitches the panoramas together so users can virtually walk down any street with imagery.

The service launched in 2007 with imagery from five US cities and has since grown to cover most public roads in roughly 100 countries. Major cities are refreshed every 2-3 years, while small towns and rural areas may have imagery several years old. Imagery dates are shown in the bottom-left of each panorama.

Beyond convenient sightseeing, Street View is used to preview hotels and rental properties, scout filming locations, confirm address signage, study city architecture, plan accessibility routes, and (for journalists and researchers) verify the location of newsworthy events from photographs.

Street View has its limits. Coverage is sparse or absent in many parts of Africa, central Asia, and the Pacific. Private roads, gated communities, and many tourist sites are excluded. Where no imagery exists, Google falls back to a normal map view; the panel below will show that fallback if your query has no Street View available.

Frequently asked questions

Why does Street View sometimes show a blurred face or license plate?

Google automatically blurs faces and license plates worldwide for privacy. The blur is applied to the imagery before publication and cannot be reversed.

Can I see indoor spaces?

Many museums, airports, train stations, restaurants, and large stores have indoor Street View available. They appear in the same interface when you walk inside a building marked with imagery dots.

How do I report a problem in an image?

From any Google Maps Street View, click the three-dot menu in the bottom right and choose Report a problem. Common reports include privacy concerns, sensitive content, or imagery in the wrong place.

Does this tool share my search with Google?

Yes. The Street View iframe is loaded directly from Google Maps, so any address or coordinate you enter is sent to Google to render the embed.
