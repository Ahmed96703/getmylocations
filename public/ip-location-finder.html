How to use this tool

  1. Look up your own IP. Click Lookup my IP. The tool fetches your public IP from a free API and displays the geolocated city, country, ISP, and an approximate map pin.
  2. Look up someone else's IP. Paste any IPv4 or IPv6 address into the input box and click Lookup. Works for any publicly routable address.
  3. Read the result carefully. The map pin is the database's best guess. The country is almost always right; the city is right roughly 50–75% of the time; the street is essentially never visible from IP alone.

What an IP lookup can and can't tell you

Every device on the public internet has a unique IP address assigned by its ISP. Geolocation databases (MaxMind, IPinfo, ipapi, BigDataCloud) map ranges of IPs to approximate cities, ISPs, and timezones based on registration data, BGP routing tables, latency probes, and crowd-sourced ground truth.

What an IP lookup reveals: the country (very reliable), region/state (usually reliable), city (often correct), ISP or mobile carrier, whether the connection is residential / mobile / hosting / VPN, and a rough latitude/longitude that is typically the centroid of the ISP's service area.

What an IP lookup does not reveal: your name, your street address, your device model, your exact GPS coordinates. To translate an IP into a real identity, an investigator would need a legal request to the ISP.

The biggest source of confusion: a VPN or corporate proxy rewrites the visible IP to the VPN's exit point. Mobile carriers route traffic through regional gateways, so cellular IPs often geolocate to a city hundreds of kilometers from where you're standing. If the city looks wrong, one of these is usually the reason — see our full IP location lookup guide for details.

Frequently asked questions

How accurate is IP geolocation?

Country level: 95–99% correct. Region/state: 80–90%. City: 50–75%. Street level: essentially never accurate. Errors of 25 km or more are common, especially on mobile networks and VPNs.

Can I look up any IP?

You can look up any publicly routable IPv4 or IPv6 address. Private addresses (10.x, 192.168.x, 172.16-31.x, fc00::/7) are not reachable from the internet and won't return useful results.

Which API does this tool use?

This tool calls ipapi.co, a free public IP geolocation API with no key required for low-volume use. Your IP and the IPs you look up are sent to that service to produce the result.

Is it legal to look someone's IP up?

Yes — public IP geolocation databases are public information. Using a lookup for harassment, stalking, or unauthorized access is illegal regardless of the lookup itself.
