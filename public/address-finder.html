How to use this tool

Address → coordinates (forward geocoding). Type any address or place name into the top box and click Find. Examples that work well: "Eiffel Tower, Paris", "1600 Pennsylvania Ave NW, Washington DC", "Karachi airport". The map flies to the result and the latitude/longitude appear in the dashboard.

Coordinates → address (reverse geocoding). Paste any pair of decimal coordinates into the second box and click Find. The closest street address, city, and country come back. Click Use my current location to fill it from your device's GPS.

What is geocoding?

Geocoding is the translation between human addresses and machine coordinates. A search box in Google Maps does forward geocoding when you type a city; a courier app does reverse geocoding when it shows the driver's location as a street name instead of a pair of numbers.

Behind the scenes, a geocoder needs three things: a database of place boundaries (countries, regions, cities, postcodes), a database of street centerlines with house-number ranges, and an algorithm that picks the most specific answer containing your input. OpenStreetMap's Nominatim service does all three from open data; commercial geocoders (Google, Mapbox, HERE) add a points-of-interest layer that resolves "Eiffel Tower" or "Burj Khalifa" to a specific building.

Accuracy depends on where you are. Cities in Western Europe, North America, and east Asia have near-perfect address coverage. Rural areas, informal settlements, and brand-new streets are often missing from any dataset. When a geocode fails, adding more context (city + country, or a nearby landmark) usually fixes it.

House-number accuracy is interpolated from street centerline ranges — so the result can be off by a few houses. For exact deliveries, surveyors and couriers use specialized address-validation services that have building-polygon ground truth.

Frequently asked questions

Why is the result slightly off from where I expected?

House-number geocoding interpolates from street centerline data. Errors of one or two houses are normal in suburbs; larger errors happen in older neighborhoods with irregular plot sizes. The map gives you a visual sanity check.

What happens if I enter a partial address?

Nominatim tries to match the most specific result it can. "Paris" returns the city of Paris. "10 Downing Street" returns the address in London. Adding city + country always helps disambiguate.

Does this tool work for non-English addresses?

Yes. Nominatim is multilingual — you can search in the local language of the address. Results may come back in the local script.

What about rate limits?

The free Nominatim service is limited to about one request per second per IP. This is fine for manual tools like this one. Heavy automated use needs a self-hosted instance.
