What are GPS coordinates?

A GPS coordinate is a pair of numbers — latitude and longitude — that pin any point on Earth to within a few meters. Latitude tells you how far north or south of the equator you are (0° to ±90°). Longitude tells you how far east or west of the Greenwich prime meridian you are (0° to ±180°). Together they form a globally unique address.

The number after the decimal point determines precision. 48.85 places you within a neighborhood; 48.858 within a street; 48.8584 within a building; 48.85842 within a parking space. Most consumer smartphones deliver about 3–5 meters of real-world accuracy under clear sky, which corresponds to five decimal places.

Modern phones don't just use the original US GPS satellites — they listen to a hybrid of GNSS constellations: GPS (USA), Galileo (EU), GLONASS (Russia), BeiDou (China), and QZSS (Japan). Listening to 20+ satellites at once is why position fixes have gotten dramatically faster and more accurate in the last few years.

How to use this tool

  1. Click Get coordinates. Your browser will ask permission the first time.
  2. Read the result. The big number is the decimal-degree pair, ready to paste into Maps. Below it, latitude / longitude appear separately in both DD and DMS formats.
  3. Check the accuracy. A value under 10 m means GPS gave a clean fix. 20–100 m means Wi-Fi positioning did. 1000+ m means IP-only — open this page outdoors for a better reading.
  4. Share it. Use the Copy button for clipboard, or click the Google Maps / OpenStreetMap links to open the exact spot.

Frequently asked questions

Why is my altitude blank?

Altitude comes from GPS only and most desktops lack a GPS chip. Even on phones, altitude is often null indoors or when the OS prefers Wi-Fi positioning. It will populate outdoors with a clear sky.

What's the difference between DD and DMS?

Decimal Degrees (e.g. 48.858420) uses a single signed number. Degrees-Minutes-Seconds (e.g. 48° 51' 30.3" N) splits each degree into 60 minutes and each minute into 60 seconds. They encode the same point. See our coordinate converter for switching between formats.

Can I get coordinates without granting GPS permission?

You can, but the accuracy drops to IP-only — typically 25 km or more. For meter-grade results, allow the precise location prompt.

Is this safe and private?

Your coordinates are processed in your browser. We do not send them to our own server. Map tiles come from OpenStreetMap, which has its own privacy practices.
