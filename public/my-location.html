How this tool works

  1. Click Find my location. Your browser asks for permission the first time. Allow it.
  2. The OS fuses signals. GPS satellites, Wi-Fi BSSID lookup, and cell-tower triangulation are combined into a single best-guess coordinate.
  3. Results display. Latitude, longitude, accuracy radius (in meters), altitude (if available), and the reverse-geocoded city / country show up in the dashboard. A pin is dropped on the map.
  4. Copy or share. One click copies the coordinates to your clipboard. Another opens the location in Google Maps for navigation.

How accurate is "my location"?

The honest answer: it depends on where you are and what hardware you have.

  • Outdoors on a phone: 3–5 meters. The GPS chip is the dominant signal.
  • Indoors on a phone: 10–25 meters. Wi-Fi positioning carries the load because the GPS signal is weak.
  • On a desktop with no GPS chip: Wi-Fi positioning (~25 m) if Apple/Google can identify your nearby access points, otherwise IP-based estimation (~25 km).
  • Permission denied or VPN active: The page falls back to pure IP geolocation, which can be off by tens of kilometers or place you in a different city entirely.

The accuracy value shown in the dashboard is the OS's own estimate of how confident it is — treat it as the radius of a circle the device believes it's inside.

If the location looks wrong, see our troubleshooting guide for step-by-step browser-specific fixes.

Frequently asked questions

Why does my browser ask for permission?

Web pages cannot read your location without your explicit consent. The permission prompt is the browser's built-in privacy guard. You can revoke it at any time from your browser's site settings.

Does it work on my computer?

Yes, but desktops usually don't have a GPS chip. The browser falls back to Wi-Fi BSSID lookup or IP geolocation, which is less precise (typically 25 m to a few km).

Why is my location wrong?

Most common reasons: you're indoors and GPS can't reach you; you're on a VPN; you're on a mobile carrier whose gateway is in a different city; or you denied the precise-location permission. Troubleshooting guide walks through each.

Is my location stored anywhere?

Your GPS coordinates are processed entirely in your browser. We do not send them to our own server. The reverse-geocoding lookup sends just the coordinates to a third party (BigDataCloud) to translate them into a city name.
